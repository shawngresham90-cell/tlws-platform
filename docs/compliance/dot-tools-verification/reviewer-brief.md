# Independent Compliance Reviewer — One-Page Brief (DOT Tools)

**Role:** independent regulatory reviewer for the TLWS DOT Tools ledger
(OWNER TO ASSIGN — may be the same reviewer engaged for the Split Sleeper
Calculator ledger; scope is compatible).

**What TLWS is building:** free driver-facing safety tools (regulation
browser, HOS pre-move check, violation/CSA-estimate lookup, pre-trip defect
guide, roadside guide, document wallet) migrating from a standalone app.
US property-carrying interstate scope. No tool renders legal conclusions —
verdicts are LOWER RISK / HIGH RISK / DO NOT MOVE / CAN'T CALL IT.

**What we need from you:**
1. Work the row-level worksheets in this folder against the primary
   sources (eCFR, FMCSA SMS methodology, FMCSA PC/yard-move guidance,
   PSP, DataQs; CVSA is reference-only per the licensing memo).
2. Sign each worksheet section; each signature flips the corresponding
   ledger rows in `../dot-tools-rule-ledger.md` from UNVERIFIED to
   VERIFIED with your name and the source date/version.
3. Flag anything the app historically got wrong (two known candidates:
   the daily-DVIR claim (R-RS-02) and the mud-flap cite (R-VIO-18)) and
   supply corrected language.
4. Review the two blocked-path proposals only if the owner asks: adverse
   conditions (worksheet section B) and, separately, the Split Calculator
   ledger's open rows (R6/R9/R15 there).

**What we are NOT asking:** no legal advice (an attorney handles the
scripts/letters separately), no software review, no CVSA reproduction —
softened non-numeric language is the standard where CVSA is the only
source.

**Ground rules:** existing app behavior counts for nothing — verify
against the source as it reads today and stamp its date/version. If a
claim can't be anchored, it doesn't ship; "everyone knows this" is not a
source. Conservative failure is always acceptable: a rule you can't
confirm becomes a CAN'T CALL IT path, not a guess.

**Recommended qualifications (owner selects; we do not nominate):** DOT
compliance consultant, former FMCSA/state MCSAP inspector or auditor, or a
transportation-safety attorney with FMCSR practice; current knowledge of
post-2020 HOS rules and the SMS methodology.

**Estimated effort:** 14 source checks, 89 ledger rows, 6 worksheets —
roughly a focused day or two, plus written notes on any mismatches.

**Deliverable:** signed worksheet sections + row-status updates (or a
marked-up copy we transcribe), and a short memo of corrections.
