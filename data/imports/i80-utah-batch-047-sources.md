# Batch 47 — I-80 Utah (Wendover/NV line → Bonneville Salt Flats → Salt Lake City → Coalville/Echo → WY line): Source Report

Every listing was web-verified during research on **2026-07-15**. No field was invented: anything a source did not state is left blank in the CSV. Coordinates are blank on every row (geocoding is a separate verified workflow). Research method: web search against official brand/operator locators (Love's, Pilot/Flying J, TA/Petro, Sinclair, CAT Scale, state DOT/port-of-entry) as primary sources, with directory/review sites (iExit, Allstays, TruckMap, Find Truck Service, truckstopsandservices.com, coopsareopen.com, state rest-area guides) as secondary confirmation.

- The ~100-mile Wendover-to-Lake Point salt-flats stretch is genuinely near-serviceless: only the Delle Sinclair (Exit 70) and two remote rest areas (Salt Flats MM10, Grassy Mountain MM55) exist across it — reported honestly, not padded.
- No national travel-center chains (Love's/Pilot/Flying J/TA/Petro) operate between Salt Lake City and the Wyoming line on I-80; SLC-area Pilot/Flying J sites are on I-15/I-215 and were excluded.
- The Grassy Mountain rest area (MM55) has no town; city recorded as **Clive**, the nearest named I-80 locality (Tooele County), to satisfy the required city field.
- West (Wendover→SLC Exit 118) and East (Coalville Exit 162→WY line) are geographically disjoint; Park City / Kimball Junction (Exits 145) had only resort/retail fuel and no genuine truck services, so none were added.
