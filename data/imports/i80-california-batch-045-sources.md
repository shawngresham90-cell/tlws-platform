# Batch 45 — I-80 California (San Francisco Bay Area → Sacramento → Donner Pass → Nevada line at Verdi): Source Report

Every listing was web-verified during research on **2026-07-15**. No field was invented: anything a source did not state is left blank in the CSV. Coordinates are blank on every row (geocoding is a separate verified workflow). Research method: web search against official brand/operator locators (Love's, Pilot/Flying J, TA/Petro, Sinclair, CAT Scale, state DOT/port-of-entry) as primary sources, with directory/review sites (iExit, Allstays, TruckMap, Find Truck Service, truckstopsandservices.com, coopsareopen.com, state rest-area guides) as secondary confirmation.

- The dense urban Bay Area stretch (SF, Oakland, Berkeley, Richmond, Vallejo) has essentially no true truck stops; the first genuine facilities appear at Fairfield/Vacaville — reported honestly, not padded.
- The Sierra/Donner Pass stretch is genuinely sparse (Truckee is the main service town); only two independent fuel stops (Cisco Grove, Dutch Flat) plus rest areas and the CHP Donner Pass scale exist.
- Cross-segment reconciliation at **Ramos Oil Dixon (Exit 66)**: returned by both the West (as a CAT-scale entry) and Central (as the truck stop) researchers. Kept once as Central's truck stop plus one clean co-located CAT-scale row; the West duplicate was dropped.
- Only facilities genuinely on mainline I-80 are included; numerous Sacramento-area stops on I-5/US-50/SR-99 were excluded per the corridor restriction. Early search summaries that fabricated Love's locations in Dixon/West Sacramento were rejected (the 4790 W Capitol site is the independent West Sac Truck Stop/ARCO).
- Two mobile/service providers with no fixed storefront (McCrary's Mobile Tire on Donner, A & T Road Service in Fairfield) are recorded at their documented corridor base cities.
